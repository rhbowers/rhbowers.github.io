The Newark Environmental Resources Inventory (ERI) is a data-driven, environmental-justice-focused report that represents a critical element in enforcing the City's Environmental Justice and Cumulative Impact Ordinance (EJCIO), passed by the City Council in 2016 thanks to efforts from local EJ advocates. The Newark ERI presents data, maps, narratives, and digital tools that provide a baseline understanding of the relationships between environment, land use, public health, and neighborhood quality of life across Newark.

As a GIS Analyst and Graduate Intern, my role included cleaning and analyzing spatial data, generating maps, and writing narratives for sections on pollution sources and natural conditions. Each month, I presented updates to and incorporated feedback from the Environmental Commission. Additionally, I led the design and development of the final ERI report in InDesign. To strengthen the impact of the ERI, I worked with staff from the Office of Sustainability and Office of Planning and Zoning to propose edits to an environmental checklist that requires developers to prove that proposed development projects do not contribute to the cumulative burden of pollution faced by vulnerable residents in Newark.
