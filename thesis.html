Project information

  • Category: Grad School
  • Thesis Title: "Exploring the Equity Implications of Green Stormwater Infrastructure Planning in Pittsburgh, PA"
  • Completion Date: April 2021
  • URL: Presentation, Thesis Dissertation
  • Applied skills: GIS, data analysis, spatial statistics, literature and policy review, equity analysis

Drawing from principles of distributive justice, my thesis uses a geospatial analytical approach to explore the equity implications of GI planning in the postindustrial city of Pittsburgh, PA. Green Infrastructure (GI) technologies offer promise in cost-effectively managing stormwater while also addressing existing social, economic, and environmental vulnerabilities. When prioritized in communities of greater privilege or in gentrifying neighborhoods, however, GI can also reinforce and reproduce existing inequities.

The findings from my thesis shine light on the equity challenges associated with sustainable climate resilience planning. As Pittsburgh begins a stormwater master planning process, this research provides critical insights for considering equity in GI planning efforts while being mindful of potential unintended consequences.
