Community-driven transformations of vacant land into spaces that serve a greater purpose are core to Grounded's mission and vision. Grounded's programs center community voices in advocating, designing, building, and maintaining outdoor spaces on vacant or neglected land. This "people and places" approach results in activated open spaces that address systemic land use issues, as well as a renewed capacity in participants to bring about greater change in their communities.

As a Project Manager for Grounded, I coordinated vacant land activation projects for the Grounded Green Stormwater Infrastructure, Green Playces, Youth Reclaim, and Airspace programs. This involved applying for grants, managing project budgets and timelines, supporting a team of project coordinators, building and maintaining relationships with project partners and contractors, organizing and leading design charrettes, presenting at public meetings, and organizing volunteer build days and celebrations. My goal was to support the development of vibrant spaces that reflected the vision of our partner communities, while remaining within project budgets and timelines.
