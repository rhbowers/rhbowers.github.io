With the goal of reducing greenhouse gas emissions from the transportation sector, several New England and Mid-Atlantic states are collaborating as part of the Transportation and Climate Initiative Program (TCI-P). The states in the TCI-P have officially committed to at least 35% of proceeds going to clean transportation projects in overburdened and underserved communities.

In partnership with the Union of Concerned Scientists, my group developed a screening tool that could help Massachusetts define and identify exactly what “overburdened and underserved” means across the Commonwealth. We designed a GIS-based tool called Massachusetts Screening System to Recognize those who are Overburdened and Underserved by Transportation, Environment, and Socioeconomic status (MassROUTES). MassROUTES uses a set of indicators grouped in three categories— Environment, Population, and Transportation— to calculate an overall score for each census tract in Massachusetts.

My role in this project was to prepare data in the environment and transportation categories. Additionally, I researched and wrote the Policy Recommendations section, which addresses the limitations in a data-driven approach to equitable policy design. I led the design and development of the final report in InDesign.
